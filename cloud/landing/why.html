WHY AIB

A standard, not a product.

AIB is the protocol-neutral identity bridge that every AI agent ecosystem needs but no single vendor can build. Open-source, W3C DID native, EU AI Act compliant by design. Built in Paris, independent from protocol vendors.

POSITIONING

What AIB is and isn't

Understanding where AIB sits in the stack prevents confusion and clarifies partnership opportunities.

✓ AIB IS a protocol bridge

A portable identity layer that sits between protocols (MCP, A2A, ANP, AG-UI) and translates credentials between them. Like a passport that works in every country.

✗ AIB is NOT an Identity Provider

We don't manage users, don't do SSO, don't do provisioning. Okta has 8,200+ integrations and 20 years of enterprise IAM. We accept their tokens via OIDC federation.

✓ AIB IS a compliance layer

Every action produces a signed receipt with intent, risk level, and EU AI Act Article 12 fields. Audit trails are cryptographic and tamper-evident.

✗ AIB is NOT a governance platform

We don't discover existing agents in your infrastructure. SailPoint scans AWS/Azure/Salesforce for shadow AI. We give agents a portable passport once they're discovered.

✓ AIB IS a W3C standard implementation

DID v1.1 (did:web + did:key), Verifiable Credentials, Ed25519VerificationKey2020. Resolvable by any Universal Resolver. Open specification.

✗ AIB is NOT a SIEM or threat detection tool

We don't correlate identity signals with SOC events. Our intent inference API detects anomalies in agent behavior, but security operations belong to dedicated tools.

EU AI ACT

Compliance by design

The EU AI Act requires complete traceability of AI system decisions. AIB receipts carry 7 structured fields that directly address Article 12 requirements.

◆ intent

What the agent is trying to accomplish. Free text, signed in the receipt. Provides the "why" behind every action.

◆ invocation_chain

Array of DIDs showing who invoked who. Traces the full chain from human request to agent action. Header X-AIB-Invoker-DID captured automatically.

◆ data_accessed

Array of datasets and APIs the agent touched during the action. Critical for data governance and GDPR Article 30 record-keeping.

◆ risk_level

Assessed risk: low, medium, high, critical. Can be set by the caller or inferred by the intent-analyze API. Filterable in audit trail queries.

⚖ human_oversight

Boolean flag: was a human in the loop? Article 14 requires human oversight for high-risk AI systems. This field proves it.

⚖ decision_rationale

Why the agent made this decision. Provides the explanability that Article 13 (transparency) requires for high-risk systems.

⚖ affected_persons

Category of persons impacted by the AI action. Required for risk assessment and fundamental rights impact assessment.

↗ Compliance Report API

GET /audit-trail?format=compliance generates a full Article 12 report: intent coverage %, human oversight %, risk breakdown, missing fields. Ready for auditors.

✦ Ed25519 Signed Receipts

Every field above is included in the Ed25519 signed receipt. Tamper-evident SHA-256 hash chain. Persistent keys encrypted AES-256. Verifiable independently without AIB servers.

PARTNERSHIPS

Better together

AIB is designed to plug into existing enterprise infrastructure, not replace it.

🔐

With Okta / Entra / Auth0

Your agents authenticate with their existing OIDC tokens. AIB verifies via JWKS and creates a portable passport. Zero migration, zero new credentials to manage.

OIDC Federation already implemented. RS256/384/512.
🛡

With SailPoint AIS

SailPoint discovers and governs your agents. AIB gives them a portable identity that works across protocols. SailPoint controls who the agent is. AIB ensures that identity is recognized everywhere.

Different layers: governance (SailPoint) vs portability (AIB).
📊

With your SIEM / SOC

AIB webhooks push signed events to your security infrastructure. Every passport creation, revocation, policy violation, and credential translation generates an HMAC-SHA256 signed notification.

4 webhook events. Real-time. Your SIEM, your rules.
